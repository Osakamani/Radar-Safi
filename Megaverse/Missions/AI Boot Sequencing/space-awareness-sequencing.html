Automatic Acceleration (A-sub-a)

Results from continuous pull (e.g. gravitational or magnetic acceleration at every infinitesimal moment) that grows or remains constant as it causes the distance e.g. between two bodies with masses A and B to shrink until the bodies collide into each other and become one body and so on. A measurable quantity to help observers determine the value of Aa is Velocity of one body e.g. B with the position of A being taken as relatively fixed. A-sub-a is defined as relative acceleration between any two bodies that is initiated by intrinsic properties / relative configurations of affected bodies. Where body A is gigantic in comparison to body B, B may not have the gravitational power to cause A to shift position but A will always affect the postion of B provided B is within range.
